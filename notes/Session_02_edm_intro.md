# Session 02: Educational Data Mining Introduction
## Session Summary
In this session, we introduced Educational Data Mining and connected it to the
student performance prediction project. We discussed three major types of EDM
questions: prediction, factor analysis, and intervention. We also finalized the six
research questions and five hypotheses that will guide the project.
## Key Concepts
- Educational Data Mining applies data mining and machine learning methods to
educational data.
- Learning analytics focuses on measuring and reporting learner data to improve
outcomes.
- Traditional statistics often explain relationships in existing data.
- Machine learning focuses on prediction and generalization to unseen cases.
- A strong research question should name the target variable, predictors, and task
type.
- A strong hypothesis should be testable and directional.
## Two-Scenario Design
The project compares two modeling scenarios:
1. Full-information scenario: includes G1 and G2 as predictors of G3.
2. Early-warning scenario: excludes G1 and G2 to avoid relying on information that
may arrive too late for intervention.
## Reflection Question
The early-warning model deliberately excludes G1 and G2, even though these
variables may improve predictive accuracy. A less accurate model may still be more
useful if it identifies academic risk early enough for educators to help students.
This trade-off shows that the model with the highest score is not always the most
useful. A useful educational model should be timely, interpretable, and actionable.
